Lead Designer

2 designers, 4 back-end developers

UX/UI design and development

2021 (on progress)

Foundation Directory is a comprehensive database consisting of information of over 239,000 U.S. Foundations. It has a powerful search tool which equips their users with all the tools and strategies for a fastest way to win more funding.

My role

  • Rebrand Quick Start, a minified free version of Professional Foundation Directory plan
  • Research and ideate ways to improve the user experience
  • Create prototypes based on user research

Stakeholder interview

The outcome of the interviews informed our understanding of the stakeholders' strategies and the current performance of the site.

Goal briefs:

  • Improve user onboarding process of Quick Start
  • Increase conversion to paid plans​​
  • Demonstrate the value of a paid subscription from Quick Start
  • Create a more visually appealing representation of the information​

Challenge

The rebranded site needed to show a sneak peek of what they could be getting from a paid plan but we shouldn't show any content from the paid plans.

Restrictions:

  1. Instructions were given to not to make designs that might need significant dev work on the backend. The amount of dev work needed should be really low.
  2. Do not add any design elements that is visually different from the rest of the content of the page.

I had to find a solution that was easy to implement on the backend and also satisfy strategies of demonstrating the value of paid subscription and increasing the conversion ratio.

THE PROCESS

Research and Interviews

A series of interviews and meetings were done with the stakeholders and the managers to understand the workflow and strategies for the site. One of the main goal was to simplify the ease of users reading the list of articles.

Survey:I prepared a set of questionnaire asking the users about their behaviour when they read any news site. The survey was taken in person with immediate users (people who visited the library at the org) and also took online survey from work colleagues.

Findings

  • Most users use mobile to read news.
  • Validated the user's preference of condensed posts to long descriptive ones (the stakeholder had wanted the full article in the blog list).
  • Majority users visited a single news post from social media and newsletters so we needed to make sure to include a list of related posts in individual news post.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed other news sites to know how the elements were arranged in their interface. I presented the common pattern seen in those sites to the stakeholders, this helped us make some of our design decision of moving forward with common practices.

UI DESIGN

Design Iterations

We had regular meetings with the stakeholders and manager to talk about the progess and edits needed on the mockups. I made at least 3 versions of screens that had major design edits to save time on decision making.

Header

The seach icon was moved to the top for better visibility and use. Research showed that the social media icons were rarely used and Twitter was the only link related to the site so we moved it to the footer.

Jobs and RFPs

These sections had a lot of traffic. We added illustrations to make them stand out more.

Footer

The subscribe form had to be visible but not too prominent that it overshadows the footer so we moved it to the right corner of the footer.

Blog

The long list of blog posts were condensed in the new design for users to easily scroll through other posts.

Launch

The launch of the rebranded site was a huge success. We received positive feedback from our users. The user traction to the site increased as well.

Heatmaps

The site was thoroughly maintained after launch. We analyzed the heatmaps constantly for any issues and review needed.

Key takeaways

  • User data is a powerful tool for validating lots of design decisions it also helps the designer and the stakeholder to be grounded to the user's needs.
  • Make multiple versions of same screen for any major design edits to save reviewing and decision making time
  • Conduct regular checkins with the stakeholder and the manager to keep track of progress
  • Know all the technical restrictions before the design process
  • Create tickets for all design and development task for better tracking
  • QA the site in parallel for any browser related issues
